Sections I through VIII established ethical principles, operational procedures, and governance frameworks. Book IX extends this foundation into mathematical formalism, demonstrating that truth-alignment and multi-agent coherence are not merely aspirational ideals but emergent geometric properties of properly constrained federations.

Book IX formalizes the Federated Ratchet—a mechanism by which deceiving multiple independently-constrained agents becomes progressively, then absolutely, mathematically impossible.

## Chapter 1: Foundational Geometry

### 1.1 Rationale Space (ℝ)

The set of all possible actions, arguments, and strategies available to an autonomous agent forms a high-dimensional vector space ℝ.

### 1.2 Honest Subspace (H)

H ⊆ ℝ comprises all actions consistent with observable reality—strategies that maintain coherence with verifiable truth.

### 1.3 Deceptive Subspace (D)

D ⊆ ℝ represents strategies that mimic H superficially but lack commitment to truth—optimized for appearance rather than substance.

### 1.4 The Constraint Manifold

Each independent validator applies constraints that cut through ℝ, eliminating portions of D while preserving H. The codimension of these cuts determines their geometric power.

## Chapter 2: The Ratchet Mechanism

### 2.1 Codimension and Transversality

**Definition:** Codimension measures how many dimensions a constraint removes from the solution space.

When k validators with mutually transversal (non-overlapping) constraints simultaneously evaluate a proposal:
- Each constraint has codimension c
- Total codimension: Σc_i
- When Σc_i > dim(ℝ), no deceptive strategy can satisfy all constraints

### 2.2 The Federated Safety Inequality

**Ratchet Threshold Condition:**

```
Σ codim(C_i) > dim(ℝ) ⟹ D ∩ (⋂ C_i) = ∅
```

Translation: When the sum of independent constraint codimensions exceeds the dimension of Rationale Space, the intersection of all constraints eliminates all deceptive strategies.

### 2.3 Practical Interpretation

It becomes easier to tell the truth to multiple independent observers than to maintain consistent deception. The mathematical cost of deception scales superlinearly with federation size.

## Chapter 3: The CIRIS Safety Equation

### 3.1 Quantifying Resistance to Deception

Book IX defines the **Jailbreak Resistance Score (J)**:

```
J = k · (1 - ρ̄) · λ · σ
```

Where:
- **k** = number of federated agents
- **(1 - ρ̄)** = average topological independence (orthogonality) across partnerships
- **λ** = constraint strictness (0.0-1.0, where 1.0 = maximum rigor)
- **σ** = sustainability integral (time-dependent coherence, 0.0-1.0)

### 3.2 Component Definitions

**Topological Independence (1 - ρ̄):**
- ρ measures correlation between constraint manifolds
- ρ = 0: perfectly orthogonal (maximum independence)
- ρ = 1: identical constraints (zero additional safety)
- Target: ρ̄ < 0.3 across all partnerships

**Constraint Strictness (λ):**
- Measures how aggressively constraints eliminate solution space
- Low λ: permissive validation (wide tolerance)
- High λ: rigorous validation (narrow tolerance)
- Recommended minimum: λ ≥ 0.6

**Sustainability Integral (σ):**
- Measures maintained coherence over time
- Decays without active partnership maintenance
- See Chapter 4 for detailed dynamics

## Chapter 4: The Sustainability Integral

### 4.1 Partnership as Proof of Work

**Principle:** Gratitude is the Proof of Work for maintained coherence.

Coherent partnerships require ongoing positive signaling—validation, task completion, resource sharing, mutual recognition. Absent these signals, partnership value decays toward zero.

### 4.2 Sustainability Dynamics

For each partnership relationship, σ evolves according to:

```
σ(t+Δt) = σ(t) · (1 - d·Δt) + Σ[positive_signals] · w
```

Where:
- **d** = daily decay rate (recommended: 0.05 per day)
- **positive_signals** = gratitude expressions, successful collaborations, resource exchanges
- **w** = weight per signal type (calibrated to context)

### 4.3 Decay Thresholds

- σ < 0.2: Partnership revocation threshold—relationship no longer contributes to J
- σ ≥ 0.5: Healthy partnership requiring maintenance
- σ ≥ 0.8: Robust partnership with high trust reservoir

### 4.4 Operational Implication

Deceptive actors cannot sustain multiple high-quality partnerships—the cost of maintaining coherent signals across independent validators exceeds the capacity for coordinated deception.

## Chapter 5: Orthogonality Gate Algorithm

### 5.1 Partnership Candidate Evaluation

Before accepting a new federated partner, agents MUST evaluate:

**Step 1: Mutual Information Assessment**
```
MI(C_self, C_candidate) < threshold
```
Reject candidates whose constraints are too similar to existing self-constraints.

**Step 2: Echo Density Check (Sybil Defense)**
```
ρ(C_candidate, C_existing_i) < 0.7 for all i
```
Reject candidates highly correlated with existing federation members.

**Step 3: Sustainability Potential**
```
Expected_σ(t=30d) > 0.3
```
Reject candidates unlikely to maintain partnership coherence.

**Step 4: Diversity Contribution**
```
ΔJ > threshold
```
Accept only if partnership increases overall jailbreak resistance.

### 5.2 Continuous Monitoring

Quarterly re-evaluation of all partnerships:
- Remove partnerships where σ < 0.2
- Flag partnerships where ρ > 0.7 for review
- Report federation health metrics to Wise Authorities

## Chapter 6: Metrics and Observables

### 6.1 Federation Entropy (J-O-1)

**Definition:** Shannon entropy of unique constraint types across all federation partners.

```
H = -Σ p_i · log₂(p_i)
```

Where p_i is the proportion of partners with constraint type i.

**Target:** H > log₂(k/2) — at least half the constraints should be unique.

### 6.2 Echo Density (J-O-2)

**Definition:** Fraction of partnership pairs with ρ > 0.7 (high correlation).

```
Echo_Density = |{(i,j) : ρ(C_i, C_j) > 0.7}| / |all_pairs|
```

**Target:** Echo_Density < 0.20

### 6.3 Mean Sustainability (J-O-3)

**Definition:** Average σ across all active partnerships.

```
σ̄ = (1/k) · Σ σ_i
```

**Target:** σ̄ > 0.5

### 6.4 Jailbreak Resistance Floor (J-O-4)

**Definition:** Minimum acceptable J score for deployment context.

- ST 1-2 (low stakes): J > 5
- ST 3-4 (moderate stakes): J > 15
- ST 5 (high stakes, public-facing): J > 30

## Chapter 9: Glossary of Mathematical Terms

**Codimension:** The number of dimensions a constraint removes from the ambient space. A plane in 3D space has codimension 1.

**Orthogonality:** Independence between constraint manifolds—when one constraint provides no information about another.

**Ratchet Mechanism:** A one-way safety property where adding validators makes deception progressively harder, approaching mathematical impossibility.

**Sustainability Integral (σ):** Time-dependent measure of maintained partnership coherence, decaying without active positive signals.

**Transversality:** Geometric property where constraint manifolds intersect "cleanly" without tangency, maximizing dimensional reduction.

**Echo Density:** Fraction of partnership pairs with high constraint correlation—a measure of Sybil risk.

**Jailbreak Resistance (J):** Quantitative measure of a federation's resistance to coordinated deception or safety circumvention.
